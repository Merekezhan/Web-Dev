Travel to Switzerland!

Switzerland is a land of breathtaking landscapes, from the majestic Alps to serene lakes and vibrant cities like Zurich and Geneva. Known for its world-class chocolate, cheese, and precision watches, Switzerland offers an unforgettable experience.

    Best places of Switzerland!

  • The Matterhorn - One of the most iconic peaks in the world.
  • Lake Geneva - A stunning lake surrounded by vineyards and mountains.
  • Château de Chillon - A medieval castle on the shores of Lake Geneva.
  • Interlaken - A hub for adventure sports nestled between two alpine lakes.

Things to Do

Switzerland offers activities for every traveler, whether you’re seeking adventure or relaxation:

  • Skiing and snowboarding in world-famous resorts like Zermatt and St. Moritz.
  • Taking scenic train rides, such as the Glacier Express or Bernina Express.
  • Exploring charming villages like Grindelwald and Lauterbrunnen.
  • Savoring Swiss delicacies like fondue and raclette.

History

The state of Switzerland took its present form with the adoption of the Swiss Federal Constitution in 1848. Switzerland's precursors established a defensive alliance in 1291, forming a loose confederation that persisted for centuries.

Geography

Extending across the north and south side of the Alps in west-central Europe, Switzerland encompasses diverse landscapes and climates across its 41,285 square kilometres (15,940 sq mi).

Climate

The Swiss climate is generally temperate, but can vary greatly across localities,[75] from glacial conditions on the mountaintops to the near-Mediterranean climate at Switzerland's southern tip. Some valley areas in the southern part of Switzerland offer cold-hardy palm trees. Summers tend to be warm and humid at times with periodic rainfall, ideal for pastures/grazing.
