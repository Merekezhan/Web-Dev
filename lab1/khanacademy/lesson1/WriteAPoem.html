Stopping by Woods on a Snowy Evening by Robert Frost

Basic information

This poem, written in 1922, is one of Robert Frost’s most famous works. It captures a moment of quiet reflection as the speaker pauses in the woods on a snowy evening. The beauty of nature, the stillness of the moment, and the speaker’s internal musings create a sense of peace and wonder.

The poem’s iconic lines:

"The woods are lovely, dark and deep,
But I have promises to keep,
And miles to go before I sleep,
And miles to go before I sleep."

These lines are often interpreted as a meditation on life’s obligations and the journey we all must continue, even when we crave rest. It’s a reminder to find beauty in the small moments but also to stay committed to our responsibilities.
